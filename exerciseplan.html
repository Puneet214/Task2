Exercise Plan

Welcome to our Exercise Plan page. Here, you can find detailed exercise routines and plans to help you achieve your fitness goals.

Beginner's Workout Plan

This is a simple workout plan designed for beginners to get started with their fitness journey.

Day 1: Full-Body Workout Pull-up:4 sets of 10 reps Squats: 3 sets of 10 reps Push-ups (knee push-ups if needed): 3 sets of 8 reps Plank: Hold for 20-30 seconds Standing Dumbbell Curls: 3 sets of 10 reps per arm
Day 2: Cardio 20-30 minutes of brisk walking or light jogging
Day 3: Full-Body Workout Lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg Bent-Over Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets of 12 reps per side Standing Dumbbell Press: 3 sets of 10 reps
Day 4: Rest or Light Activity
Day 5: Cardio 20-30 minutes of cycling or swimming
Day 6: Full-Body Workout Step-Ups: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg Tricep Dips: 3 sets of 10 reps Plank: Hold for 20-30 seconds Bicep Curls with Resistance Bands: 3 sets of 10 reps Day 7: Rest

Intermediate Workout Plan

If you've been working out for a while and want to take your fitness to the next level, this plan is for you.

Day 1: Upper Body Strength Bench Press: 3 sets of 8 reps Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets of 8 reps Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps per arm Push-Ups: 3 sets of 12 reps Plank: Hold for 30-45 seconds
Day 2: Cardio 30-40 minutes of running or high-intensity interval training (HIIT)
Day 3: Lower Body Strength Squats: 3 sets of 10 reps Deadlifts: 3 sets of 8 reps Lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg Leg Press: 3 sets of 10 reps Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets of 15 reps per side
Day 4: Rest or Light Activity
Day 5: Full-Body Workout Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets of 8 reps Dumbbell Bench Press: 3 sets of 10 reps Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets of 8 reps Plank: Hold for 30-45 seconds
Day 6: Cardio 40-50 minutes of cycling or swimming
Day 7: Rest Remember to warm up before each workout and cool down afterward. Start with weights and repetitions that are challenging but doable, and gradually increase the intensity and weight as you become more comfortable with the exercises. Listen to your body, and if you experience pain or discomfort, consult with a fitness professional.
